Read me first

This walkthrough builds a rough estimate of your {{ tax_year }} federal and state income taxes from a handful of numbers. It never touches your data — nothing you type here is saved.

Hard caveats — please actually read these:
  • This is not tax advice; no CPA or financial advisor was involved. Estimates only.
  • Rates are for tax year {{ tax_year }} and may change — verify before relying on results.
  • State math is approximate (flat rate × a federal base). The results page shows exactly which approximation was used for your state.
  • Not modeled: AMT, capital-loss netting, self-employment losses, QBI, most credits and phase-outs, local/county taxes.

Your income types

Sole proprietor / single-member LLC: you'll enter your net self-employment profit (Schedule C line 31). The tool automatically applies SE tax on 92.35% of that profit and the half-SE-tax deduction — don't subtract either yourself. The half-SE-tax deduction is a 1040 deduction, not a business expense, so your Schedule C profit does not already include it.
S-corp owner: your salary belongs in the W-2 fields (toggle "W-2 job" above and enter it there — FICA is already withheld on it). Your owner distributions are not subject to SE tax — you'll get a separate "owner distributions" field, taxed as ordinary income with no SE tax.

Your deductions

Don't double-count payroll pre-tax items. 401(k) deferrals and health insurance premiums taken out of your paycheck are usually already excluded from your W-2 Box 1 wages (they show up in Box 12 — code D for 401(k) — not in Box 1). Quick self-check on last year's W-2: if Box 3 is bigger than Box 1, the difference is your pre-tax deferrals, already excluded. Entering them again here would deduct them twice. Only toggle IRA/HSA for money you contributed outside payroll.
