FICA is one combined line here (Social Security 6.2% + Medicare 1.45% — paystubs often show them together). If your stub splits them, add them up. Retirement — pre-tax 401k and Roth/post-tax alike — books as a transfer into your own retirement account (it's savings, not income lost; if you have both, two transfer rows share the account below). Garnishment / other post-tax is money you earned that was taken after tax — it books as contra-income under its own label, so gross stays truthful. The Other adjustment field is a plug for anything else on the stub (rounding, one-off lines) so the math ties; a negative value works for extra income.
