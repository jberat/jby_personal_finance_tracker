About payroll reconciliation

Your bank import only shows the net direct deposit — the number left after your employer withheld taxes and pre-tax deductions. That understates what you actually earned and hides where a third or more of your pay went. This tool books each paycheck at gross: it splits the deposit into your full wages (income), offsetting lines for federal/state tax, FICA, and pre-tax health premiums, and a transfer for any 401k/retirement contribution — that one is your own money moving into your retirement account (and it can sync straight into Investments), not income lost. Post-tax lines get the same care: Roth / post-tax retirement is also a transfer into your retirement account (savings, just taxed first), while a garnishment or other post-tax deduction books as contra-income — money you earned that was then taken. The lines still sum to net, so your cash totals don't change — but your income pages now show real earnings and real taxes paid.

Two ways in, same result. If your employer runs Gusto, upload the Payroll Journal Report CSV and every pay period parses automatically. Otherwise, type the numbers off any paystub — one paycheck at a time, or a single year-to-date entry if you'd rather true-up annually. Every reconciliation is saved as a draft first, shows you exactly what it will book, and can be undone later.
